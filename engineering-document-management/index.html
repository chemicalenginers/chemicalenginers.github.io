Engineering document management is the systematic control of engineering documents and drawings throughout their lifecycle. It involves creating, storing, indexing, securing, and distributing these documents to authorized users. An effective engineering document management system can help organizations improve productivity, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.

Engineering documents are essential for the design, construction, and maintenance of products and infrastructure. They contain valuable information that can be used to make informed decisions, avoid errors, and ensure compliance with regulations. However, managing these documents can be a complex and time-consuming task, especially for large organizations with a high volume of documents.

An engineering document management system can help organizations overcome these challenges by providing a centralized repository for all engineering documents. This makes it easy to find, access, and share documents with authorized users. The system can also help organizations track changes to documents, manage revisions, and control access to sensitive information.

Engineering Document Management

Engineering document management is a critical aspect of product development and manufacturing. It ensures that engineering documents are created, stored, and managed in a way that supports efficient collaboration and decision-making.

  • Centralized repository: A single source of truth for all engineering documents.
  • Document control: Processes to ensure that documents are accurate, up-to-date, and approved.
  • Revision control: Tracking changes to documents over time.
  • Access control: Restricting access to documents based on user permissions.
  • Document search: Quickly finding the documents you need.
  • Collaboration: Enabling multiple users to work on documents simultaneously.
  • Security: Protecting documents from unauthorized access and modification.
  • Compliance: Ensuring that documents meet regulatory requirements.
  • Integration: Connecting with other systems, such as PLM and ERP.
  • Reporting: Generating reports on document usage and activity.

These aspects of engineering document management are essential for ensuring that engineering documents are accurate, up-to-date, and accessible to the people who need them. By implementing effective engineering document management practices, organizations can improve productivity, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.

Centralized repository


A centralized repository is a key component of engineering document management. It provides a single, authoritative source of truth for all engineering documents, ensuring that everyone is working with the most up-to-date information. This can help to improve productivity, reduce errors, and mitigate risks.

For example, if an engineer is working on a design change, they can be confident that they are using the latest version of the drawing. This can help to prevent errors and ensure that the change is implemented correctly. A centralized repository can also help to reduce the risk of losing documents or having multiple versions of the same document floating around.

In addition, a centralized repository can make it easier to track changes to documents and manage revisions. This can be important for ensuring that documents are accurate and up-to-date, and for tracking the history of changes made to documents.

Overall, a centralized repository is an essential component of engineering document management. It can help to improve productivity, reduce errors, and mitigate risks.

Document control


Document control is a critical aspect of engineering document management. It ensures that engineering documents are created, reviewed, and approved according to established procedures. This helps to ensure that documents are accurate, up-to-date, and meet the needs of the organization.

  • Review and approval: All engineering documents should be reviewed and approved by authorized personnel before they are released. This helps to ensure that the documents are accurate, complete, and meet the organization's standards.
  • Document change control: Engineering documents often change over time. It is important to have a process in place to control changes to documents and ensure that all changes are properly reviewed and approved.
  • Document version control: Engineering documents should be version controlled to ensure that everyone is working with the latest version of the document. This helps to prevent errors and confusion.
  • Document storage and retrieval: Engineering documents should be stored in a secure location where they can be easily retrieved when needed. This helps to ensure that documents are not lost or damaged.

Effective document control is essential for ensuring that engineering documents are accurate, up-to-date, and meet the needs of the organization. By implementing effective document control processes, organizations can improve productivity, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.

Revision control


Revision control is an essential aspect of engineering document management. It allows organizations to track changes to documents over time, ensuring that everyone is working with the latest version of the document and that changes are properly reviewed and approved.

  • Document history: Revision control systems track the history of changes to documents, allowing users to see who made changes, when changes were made, and what changes were made.
  • Collaboration: Revision control systems allow multiple users to work on documents simultaneously, tracking changes and merging them together.
  • Version control: Revision control systems allow organizations to control which versions of documents are released and used, ensuring that everyone is working with the latest approved version.
  • Compliance: Revision control systems can help organizations comply with regulatory requirements that mandate the tracking of changes to documents.

Effective revision control is essential for ensuring that engineering documents are accurate, up-to-date, and meet the needs of the organization. By implementing effective revision control processes, organizations can improve productivity, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.

Access control


Access control is an essential aspect of engineering document management. It ensures that only authorized users have access to sensitive engineering documents. This can help to protect intellectual property, prevent unauthorized modifications, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

There are a number of different ways to implement access control for engineering documents. One common approach is to use a role-based access control (RBAC) system. RBAC allows administrators to define roles and assign permissions to those roles. Users are then assigned to roles, and they inherit the permissions associated with those roles.

For example, an administrator might create a role called "Engineer" and assign permissions to that role that allow engineers to create, edit, and delete engineering documents. The administrator might then create a role called "Viewer" and assign permissions to that role that allow viewers to view engineering documents but not edit or delete them.

Access control is a critical component of engineering document management. It helps to protect sensitive information, prevent unauthorized modifications, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. By implementing effective access control measures, organizations can improve productivity, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.

Document search


Document search is a critical aspect of engineering document management. It allows users to quickly and easily find the documents they need, which can save time and improve productivity.

  • Keyword search: Keyword search is a simple and effective way to find documents. Users can enter one or more keywords into a search field, and the system will return a list of documents that contain those keywords.
  • Full-text search: Full-text search is a more comprehensive search method that allows users to search for words or phrases anywhere in a document. This can be useful for finding documents that contain specific information, even if the user does not know the exact location of the information.
  • Metadata search: Metadata search allows users to search for documents based on their metadata, such as the author, date created, or file type. This can be useful for finding documents that meet specific criteria, such as documents that were created by a particular author or that were created within a specific date range.
  • Attribute search: Attribute search allows users to search for documents based on their attributes, such as the document type, status, or project. This can be useful for finding documents that are related to a specific project or that are of a specific type.

By providing users with a variety of search options, engineering document management systems can help users quickly and easily find the documents they need. This can save time and improve productivity.

  • Knowledge sharing: Collaboration allows users to share knowledge and expertise with each other. This can help to improve the overall quality of engineering documents.
  • Risk mitigation: Collaboration can help to mitigate risks by allowing multiple users to review and approve documents. This can help to ensure that documents are accurate and complete, and that they meet all regulatory requirements.

Overall, collaboration is an essential aspect of engineering document management. It can help to save time, improve productivity, and reduce risks.

Compliance


Compliance is a critical aspect of engineering document management. It ensures that engineering documents meet all applicable regulatory requirements. This is important for protecting public health and safety, as well as for avoiding legal penalties.

There are a number of different regulatory requirements that may apply to engineering documents. These requirements can vary depending on the industry and the jurisdiction. Some common regulatory requirements include:

  • ISO 9001: This is an international standard that sets out the requirements for a quality management system. ISO 9001 requires organizations to have a system in place to control and manage their documents.
  • ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code: This is a code that sets out the requirements for the design, construction, and inspection of boilers and pressure vessels. The ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code requires that all engineering documents related to boilers and pressure vessels be reviewed and approved by a qualified engineer.
  • FDA 21 CFR Part 11: This is a regulation that sets out the requirements for electronic records and signatures in the pharmaceutical industry. FDA 21 CFR Part 11 requires that all electronic engineering documents be signed by an authorized person and that the documents be stored in a secure manner.

Engineering document management systems can help organizations to comply with regulatory requirements by providing tools and features to control and manage documents. For example, engineering document management systems can be used to:

  • Track changes to documents: This helps to ensure that all changes to documents are reviewed and approved by authorized personnel.
  • Control access to documents: This helps to ensure that only authorized personnel have access to sensitive engineering documents.
  • Store documents in a secure manner: This helps to protect documents from unauthorized access and modification.

By implementing an effective engineering document management system, organizations can improve their compliance with regulatory requirements. This can help to protect public health and safety, as well as avoid legal penalties.
