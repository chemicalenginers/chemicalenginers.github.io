FAQs on Mechanical Engineering Online

The following are frequently asked questions (FAQs) about mechanical engineering online programs:

Question 1: Are online mechanical engineering programs accredited?

Yes, many online mechanical engineering programs are accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET (EAC of ABET). Accreditation ensures that a program meets the quality standards established by the engineering profession.

Question 2: Are online mechanical engineering programs as good as traditional on-campus programs?

Yes, online mechanical engineering programs can be just as good as traditional on-campus programs. Online programs provide students with access to the same curriculum, faculty, and resources as on-campus programs. In some cases, online programs may even offer more flexibility and convenience than on-campus programs.

Question 3: Are there any prerequisites for online mechanical engineering programs?

Yes, most online mechanical engineering programs have prerequisites, such as a bachelor's degree in engineering or a related field. Some programs may also require students to have completed certain math and science courses.

Question 4: How long does it take to complete an online mechanical engineering program?

The time it takes to complete an online mechanical engineering program varies depending on the program and the student's pace of study. However, most programs can be completed in 2-3 years.

Question 5: How much does an online mechanical engineering program cost?

The cost of an online mechanical engineering program varies depending on the program and the institution offering it. However, online programs are often more affordable than traditional on-campus programs.

Question 6: What are the benefits of earning a mechanical engineering degree online?

There are many benefits to earning a mechanical engineering degree online, including flexibility, affordability, and accessibility. Online programs allow students to learn at their own pace and on their own schedule, which can be beneficial for students who have busy schedules or who live in remote areas.

Online programs offer flexibility, but it's important to set realistic goals for your studies. Create a study schedule that works for you and stick to it as much as possible.
