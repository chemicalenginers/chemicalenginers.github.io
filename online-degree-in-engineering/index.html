Question 1: What is an online degree in engineering?


An online degree in engineering is a degree that is earned through online learning. Students take courses online, complete assignments online, and participate in online discussions. They do not have to attend any classes on campus.

Question 2: Are online engineering degrees as good as traditional on-campus degrees?


Yes, online engineering degrees are as good as traditional on-campus degrees. In fact, some studies have shown that online students perform just as well as on-campus students on standardized tests.

Question 3: What are the benefits of earning an online engineering degree?

  • Variety: Online engineering programs offer a wide variety of courses and specializations.
  • Career advancement: An online engineering degree can help you advance your career.

Question 4: What are the drawbacks of earning an online engineering degree?


There are some drawbacks to earning an online engineering degree, including:

  • Lack of face-to-face interaction: Online students do not have the same opportunities to interact with their professors and classmates as on-campus students.
  • Technical difficulties: Online students may experience technical difficulties, such as internet outages or computer problems.
  • Self-motivation: Online students must be self-motivated to succeed.

Question 5: Am I a good candidate for an online engineering degree?


If you are a self-motivated learner who is comfortable with technology, then you may be a good candidate for an online engineering degree. You should also have a strong foundation in math and science.

Choosing the right online degree program in engineering is an important decision. To help you make the best choice for your needs, here are five tips:
