Accreditation


Accreditation is a critical factor to consider when choosing an online engineering degree program. Accreditation ensures that your degree meets the high standards set by the engineering profession and that it will be recognized by employers. There are a number of different engineering accreditation bodies, but the most well-known and respected is the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET). ABET accredits engineering programs at all levels, from associate's degrees to doctoral degrees.

  • Why is accreditation important?

    Accreditation is important for several reasons. First, it ensures that your degree meets the high standards set by the engineering profession. This means that you will have the knowledge and skills that employers are looking for. Second, accreditation can help you to get licensed as a professional engineer. In most states, you must have a degree from an accredited program in order to be eligible for licensure. Third, accreditation can help you to advance your career. Many employers give preference to candidates with degrees from accredited programs.

  • How can I find out if a program is accredited?

    You can find out if a program is accredited by visiting the ABET website. ABET has a searchable database of all accredited engineering programs.

  • What should I do if I have a degree from a non-accredited program?

    If you have a degree from a non-accredited program, you may still be able to get licensed as a professional engineer. However, you may need to take additional coursework or pass a special exam.

Accreditation is an important factor to consider when choosing an online engineering degree program. By choosing an accredited program, you can ensure that your degree will be recognized by employers and that you will have the knowledge and skills you need to succeed in your career.
