Project Management


Project management is a critical component of engineering management, as it provides the skills needed to plan, execute, and control engineering projects. These skills are essential for ensuring that projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

The engineering management program at ASU provides students with a strong foundation in project management, including the following key concepts:

  • Project planning: This involves defining the scope of the project, identifying the tasks that need to be completed, and developing a timeline for the project.
  • Project execution: This involves managing the day-to-day activities of the project, including assigning tasks to team members, tracking progress, and ensuring that the project is on track.
  • Project control: This involves monitoring the project's progress and taking corrective action when necessary to ensure that the project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

Students in the engineering management program at ASU gain practical experience in project management through a variety of assignments and projects. For example, students may be required to develop a project plan for a new product launch or to manage a team of students in the design and construction of a prototype.
