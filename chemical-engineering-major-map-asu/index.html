Core Coursework


Core coursework in chemical engineering principles forms the backbone of the chemical engineering major map at ASU. These foundational courses provide students with a deep understanding of the fundamental concepts and theories that underpin the field of chemical engineering.

  • Thermodynamics: The study of energy, heat, and work, and their relation to physical and chemical systems. In chemical engineering, thermodynamics is used to design and optimize processes involving energy conversion and transfer.
  • Fluid mechanics: The study of the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) at rest and in motion. Chemical engineers use fluid mechanics to design and operate systems for fluid transport, mixing, and separation.
  • Heat and mass transfer: The study of the transfer of heat and mass between different systems. Chemical engineers apply heat and mass transfer principles in the design of heat exchangers, reactors, and other process equipment.

These core courses provide students with the essential knowledge and skills needed to succeed in the field of chemical engineering. They lay the foundation for more advanced coursework in specialized areas such as biochemical engineering, materials science, and environmental engineering.
