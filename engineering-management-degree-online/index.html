Engineering Economics


Engineering economics is a critical component of engineering management. It involves analyzing the economic feasibility of engineering projects to ensure that they are financially sound and will provide a positive return on investment.

  • Cost-benefit analysis: This technique is used to compare the costs and benefits of an engineering project to determine its economic feasibility. A positive cost-benefit ratio indicates that the project is likely to be profitable.
  • Life-cycle costing: This technique considers the total cost of an engineering project over its entire life cycle, including initial investment, operating costs, and maintenance costs. This information can be used to make informed decisions about the long-term viability of a project.
  • Risk analysis: This technique is used to identify and assess the risks associated with an engineering project. This information can be used to develop mitigation strategies and make informed decisions about the project's feasibility.
  • Capital budgeting: This technique is used to evaluate and select long-term investment projects. This information can be used to make informed decisions about which projects to pursue and how to allocate capital.

By completing an engineering management degree online, students can gain the knowledge and skills necessary to analyze the economic feasibility of engineering projects. This information can be used to make informed decisions about which projects to pursue and how to allocate resources. This can help organizations to improve their efficiency, reduce costs, and increase profitability.

Risk Management


Risk management is a critical component of engineering management. It involves identifying and mitigating risks in engineering projects to ensure that they are completed successfully.

  • Risk Identification: The first step in risk management is to identify potential risks that could impact an engineering project. This can be done through brainstorming, risk workshops, and historical data analysis.
  • Risk Assessment: Once risks have been identified, they need to be assessed to determine their likelihood and impact. This can be done using a variety of risk assessment techniques, such as qualitative risk analysis and quantitative risk analysis.
  • Risk Mitigation: Once risks have been assessed, they need to be mitigated to reduce their likelihood and impact. This can be done through a variety of risk mitigation strategies, such as avoidance, mitigation, and contingency planning.
  • Risk Monitoring: Once risks have been mitigated, they need to be monitored to ensure that they are being managed effectively. This can be done through regular risk reviews and updates.

By completing an engineering management degree online, students can gain the knowledge and skills necessary to identify, assess, mitigate, and monitor risks in engineering projects. This can help organizations to improve their efficiency, reduce costs, and increase the likelihood of project success.

Tips for Pursuing an Engineering Management Degree Online

Earning an engineering management degree online can be a great way to advance your career while balancing your work and personal commitments. However, there are a few things you should keep in mind to ensure your success.

Tip 1: Choose an Accredited Program

Accreditation is important because it ensures that your degree will be recognized by employers and professional organizations. When choosing a program, look for one that is accredited by a reputable accrediting body, such as the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET).

Tip 2: Make Sure the Program Fits Your Needs

Not all engineering management degree programs online are created equal. Some programs are designed for working professionals, while others are more research-focused. Consider your career goals and make sure the program you choose aligns with your interests.

Tip 3: Be Prepared to Work Hard

Earning an engineering management degree online requires a significant amount of hard work and dedication. Be prepared to spend several hours each week studying, completing assignments, and participating in online discussions.

Tip 4: Take Advantage of Resources

Most online programs offer a variety of resources to help students succeed. These resources may include online tutoring, technical support, and career counseling. Take advantage of these resources whenever you need them.

Tip 5: Network with Other Students

Networking with other students is a great way to learn from your peers and build relationships that can help you in your career. Participate in online forums and discussion groups, and attend virtual events to connect with other students.

Tip 6: Don't Be Afraid to Ask for Help

If you're struggling with a particular concept or assignment, don't be afraid to ask for help from your professors, classmates, or teaching assistants. Most people are willing to help, so don't be shy about reaching out.
