ASU Human Systems Engineering

Human Systems Engineering (HSE) is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on the design, development, and integration of systems that involve humans. It's a crucial field that encompasses various dimensions, as highlighted by the following key aspects:

  • Human-centered design: Designing systems that prioritize the needs and capabilities of the user.

Human-centered design


Human-centered design is a fundamental principle of ASU Human Systems Engineering. It's a design philosophy that focuses on understanding and meeting the needs of the people who will use a system. This user-centric approach ensures that systems are safe, efficient, effective, and easy to use.

  • Understanding user needs: Human systems engineers conduct user research to understand the needs, goals, and limitations of the people who will use a system. This research can involve surveys, interviews, observations, and focus groups.
  • Designing for usability: Human systems engineers design systems that are easy to use and understand. They consider factors such as the user's cognitive abilities, physical limitations, and cultural background.
  • Iterative design: Human systems engineers use an iterative design process to develop systems. This process involves creating prototypes, testing them with users, and making improvements based on feedback.
  • User-centered evaluation: Human systems engineers evaluate systems throughout the design process to ensure that they meet the needs of users. This evaluation can involve usability testing, field studies, and other methods.

Human-centered design is essential for creating systems that are safe, efficient, effective, and user-friendly. By focusing on the needs and capabilities of the user, ASU Human Systems Engineering professionals can develop systems that improve the quality of life for everyone.

Systems Thinking


Systems thinking is a critical component of ASU Human Systems Engineering. It is a way of understanding the complex interactions between humans and systems. By taking a systems approach, engineers can design systems that are more effective, efficient, and safe.

One of the key challenges in systems engineering is understanding how humans interact with systems. Humans are complex beings, and their behavior can be unpredictable. This can make it difficult to design systems that are safe and effective. However, by taking a systems approach, engineers can identify and address potential hazards and inefficiencies.

For example, in the design of a new medical device, engineers must consider how the device will be used by both patients and healthcare providers. They must also consider how the device will interact with other systems in the hospital, such as the electronic health record system. By taking a systems approach, engineers can design a device that is safe, effective, and easy to use.

Systems thinking is also essential for the design of complex systems, such as transportation systems and power grids. These systems are made up of many different components, and it is important to understand how these components interact with each other. By taking a systems approach, engineers can design systems that are more resilient and efficient.

Overall, systems thinking is a powerful tool that can be used to design safer, more efficient, and more effective systems. By understanding the complex interactions between humans and systems, engineers can create systems that improve the quality of life for everyone.

Cognitive engineering


Cognitive engineering is a branch of human factors engineering that focuses on understanding how human cognition affects system design. Cognitive engineers work to design systems that are easy to use, efficient, and safe. They consider factors such as human memory, attention, and decision-making when designing systems.

  • Mental models: Mental models are representations of the world that people use to understand and interact with systems. Cognitive engineers can use mental models to design systems that are consistent with the way that people think.
  • Workload: Workload is the amount of mental effort required to use a system. Cognitive engineers can design systems that minimize workload and help users to avoid errors.
  • Situation awareness: Situation awareness is the ability to understand the current state of a system and to anticipate future events. Cognitive engineers can design systems that provide users with the information they need to maintain situation awareness.
  • Decision-making: Decision-making is the process of choosing between different options. Cognitive engineers can design systems that support decision-making by providing users with clear and concise information.

Cognitive engineering is an essential part of ASU Human Systems Engineering. By understanding how human cognition affects system design, cognitive engineers can design systems that are safe, efficient, and easy to use.
