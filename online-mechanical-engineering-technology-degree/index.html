ABET accreditation


ABET accreditation is a voluntary, non-governmental certification process that assures that a college or university program meets the quality standards of the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET). ABET accreditation is recognized by most major employers in the field of engineering and technology, and it is often a requirement for licensure as a professional engineer.

  • What are the benefits of ABET accreditation for students?

    There are several benefits of ABET accreditation for students, including:

    • ABET accreditation ensures that students are receiving a high-quality education that meets the standards of the engineering profession.
    • ABET accreditation can help students get licensed as professional engineers.
    • ABET accreditation can make students more competitive in the job market.
  • What are the benefits of ABET accreditation for employers?

    There are also several benefits of ABET accreditation for employers, including:

    • ABET accreditation ensures that employers are hiring graduates who have received a high-quality education.
    • ABET accreditation can help employers reduce their training costs.
    • ABET accreditation can help employers improve their productivity.

Overall, ABET accreditation is a valuable asset for both students and employers. Students who graduate from ABET-accredited programs are more likely to be successful in their careers, and employers who hire ABET-accredited graduates are more likely to be successful in their businesses.
