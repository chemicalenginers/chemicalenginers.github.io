Firstly, it enhances the employability of graduates. Employers seek candidates with qualifications that are recognized and respected within the engineering industry. Secondly, it facilitates professional development and career advancement. Professional organizations, such as the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) and the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME), often require members to hold accredited degrees. These organizations provide opportunities for continuing education, networking, and professional development, which are essential for career growth. By obtaining an accredited online engineering degree, graduates can meet the eligibility criteria for these organizations and unlock the benefits associated with membership.

Thirdly, it ensures the credibility and trustworthiness of online engineering programs. Accreditation serves as an independent verification that the program adheres to recognized standards of quality. This assurance is particularly important in the context of online education, where there may be concerns about the validity and rigor of programs offered remotely. The recognition of online engineering degrees by accreditation bodies provides confidence to students, employers, and the wider community that the education and training received meet industry standards.
