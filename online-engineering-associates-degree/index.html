Online Engineering Associate's Degree FAQs

This section addresses frequently asked questions (FAQs) about online engineering associate's degrees, providing concise and informative answers to common concerns or misconceptions.

Question 1: Are online engineering associate's degrees as valuable as traditional on-campus degrees?

Answer: Yes, online engineering associate's degrees are considered just as valuable as traditional on-campus degrees by employers. They provide students with the same foundational knowledge and skills, and graduates are equally prepared for entry-level engineering positions.

Question 2: Can I transfer credits from my online engineering associate's degree to a bachelor's degree program?

Answer: Yes, many online engineering associate's degrees are designed to be transferable to bachelor's degree programs. Students should work closely with their academic advisor to ensure that their coursework will align with their intended bachelor's degree program.

Question 3: Are there any prerequisites for online engineering associate's degree programs?

Answer: Prerequisites vary depending on the program, but most require a high school diploma or equivalent and some college-level math and science courses. Some programs may also require specific engineering coursework.

Question 4: What types of jobs can I get with an online engineering associate's degree?

Answer: Graduates with an online engineering associate's degree can pursue a variety of entry-level engineering positions, such as engineering technician, drafter, or quality control inspector. They may work in various industries, including manufacturing, construction, and energy.

Question 5: Is it possible to complete an online engineering associate's degree while working full-time?

Answer: Yes, many online engineering associate's degree programs are designed for working adults. They offer flexible scheduling and part-time study options, allowing students to balance their education with their work and personal commitments.

Question 6: How long does it take to complete an online engineering associate's degree?

Answer: The completion time for an online engineering associate's degree varies depending on the program and the student's pace of study. However, most programs can be completed in two to three years of full-time study.
