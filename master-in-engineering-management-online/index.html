Project Management


Project Management is a critical component of a Master's in Engineering Management (MEM) online. Engineering managers are responsible for planning, executing, and controlling projects, so they need to have a strong understanding of project management principles and practices.

  • Planning

    The planning phase of a project involves identifying the project's goals, objectives, and scope. The project manager also needs to develop a project plan that outlines the steps that need to be taken to complete the project. The project manager should define milestones and a work structure to track the progress of the project.

  • Execution

    The execution phase of a project involves carrying out the project plan. The project manager is responsible for ensuring that the project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

  • Control

    The control phase of a project involves monitoring the project's progress and making any necessary adjustments. The project manager should use Earned Value Management (EVM) to track the project's performance against the plan. The project manager can use this information to identify any potential problems and take corrective action.

  • Stakeholder Management

    Stakeholder management is a critical part of project management. The project manager needs to identify all of the stakeholders in the project and manage their expectations. The project manager should communicate regularly with stakeholders to keep them informed of the project's progress and to address any concerns.

Project management is a complex and challenging field, but it is essential for engineering managers. MEM programs provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to be successful project managers.

Engineering Economics


Engineering economics is a critical component of a Master's in Engineering Management (MEM) online. Engineering managers need to be able to make sound financial decisions in order to ensure the success of their projects. Engineering economics provides the tools and techniques that engineers need to evaluate the economic feasibility of projects and make informed decisions about how to allocate resources.

  • Cost-Benefit Analysis

    Cost-benefit analysis is a technique used to evaluate the economic feasibility of a project. It involves comparing the costs of a project to the benefits that it will generate. Engineering managers use cost-benefit analysis to determine whether a project is worth pursuing.

  • Life-Cycle Costing

    Life-cycle costing is a technique used to evaluate the total cost of owning and operating a product or system over its entire lifetime. Engineering managers use life-cycle costing to make decisions about which products or systems to purchase.

  • Risk Analysis

    Risk analysis is a technique used to identify and assess the risks associated with a project. Engineering managers use risk analysis to develop strategies to mitigate risks and protect their projects from financial losses.

  • Decision Analysis

    Decision analysis is a technique used to make decisions under uncertainty. Engineering managers use decision analysis to make decisions about which projects to pursue and how to allocate resources.

Engineering economics is a powerful tool that can help engineering managers make sound financial decisions. MEM programs provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to use engineering economics effectively in their careers.

Quality Control


Quality control plays a pivotal role in engineering management, ensuring that projects meet the highest standards of quality and efficiency. A Master's in Engineering Management (MEM) online provides students with the knowledge and skills necessary to implement and maintain effective quality control systems within engineering projects.

  • Establishing Quality Standards

  • Inspection and Testing

  • Corrective Action

By mastering quality control principles and practices, MEM graduates are well-equipped to lead and manage engineering projects that deliver high-quality results, meet customer requirements, and contribute to the overall success of the organization.

Operations Research


Operations research (OR) is the application of analytical methods to improve decision-making. OR techniques are used in a wide range of industries, including engineering, manufacturing, transportation, and healthcare. A Master's in Engineering Management (MEM) online provides students with the knowledge and skills to use OR techniques to solve complex engineering problems.

OR is a valuable tool for engineering managers because it can help them to:

  • Improve efficiency and productivity
  • Reduce costs
  • Make better decisions
  • Manage risk

For example, OR techniques can be used to design efficient production schedules, optimize inventory levels, and improve customer service. OR techniques can also be used to evaluate the risks and benefits of different engineering projects.

MEM programs typically include courses in OR, such as linear programming, integer programming, and simulation. These courses provide students with the foundation they need to use OR techniques to solve real-world problems.

OR is an essential tool for engineering managers who want to be successful in their careers. MEM programs provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to use OR techniques effectively.
