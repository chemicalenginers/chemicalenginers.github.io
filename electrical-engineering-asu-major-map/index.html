Core Courses


Core courses are the foundation of an electrical engineering ASU major map. They provide students with the essential knowledge and skills needed to succeed in more advanced coursework and in their future careers. Circuit analysis, digital systems, and electromagnetics are three of the most important core courses for electrical engineers. Circuit analysis provides students with the foundation for understanding how electrical circuits work. Digital systems introduces students to the design and analysis of digital circuits, which are used in everything from computers to cell phones. Electromagnetics provides students with the foundation for understanding how electrical and magnetic fields interact.

These core courses are essential for electrical engineers because they provide the foundation for understanding more advanced topics such as power systems, control systems, and microelectronics. Without a strong foundation in these core courses, students would not be able to succeed in more advanced coursework or in their careers.

For example, a student who wants to design power systems needs to have a strong understanding of circuit analysis. A student who wants to design control systems needs to have a strong understanding of digital systems. And a student who wants to design microelectronics needs to have a strong understanding of electromagnetics.

The electrical engineering ASU major map is designed to ensure that students take the core courses they need to succeed in their careers. By taking these courses in the proper sequence, students can build a strong foundation in electrical engineering fundamentals and prepare themselves for success in more advanced coursework and in their future careers.
