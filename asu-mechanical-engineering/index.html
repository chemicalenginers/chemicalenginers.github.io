ABET accredited


ABET accreditation is a significant indicator of the quality of an engineering program. It signifies that the program meets the high standards set by ABET, which is the recognized accreditor for engineering programs in the United States.

  • Ensures program quality: ABET accreditation ensures that the program provides students with a high-quality education that meets the needs of the engineering profession. The accreditation process involves a rigorous evaluation of the program's curriculum, faculty, and facilities.
  • Recognized by employers: ABET accreditation is recognized by employers as a mark of quality. Employers know that graduates of ABET-accredited programs have the skills and knowledge necessary to be successful in the field.
  • Enhances graduate opportunities: Graduates of ABET-accredited programs are eligible to sit for the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam, which is the first step towards becoming a licensed professional engineer. They are also more likely to be accepted into graduate schools and research programs.
  • Continuous improvement: ABET accreditation is a continuous process. Programs must undergo a periodic review to maintain their accreditation. This process ensures that the program is constantly improving and meeting the changing needs of the profession.
